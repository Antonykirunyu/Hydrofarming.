HYDROPONIC FARMING.

Hydroponic farming is a high-tech growing method, where vegetables are grown without soil. The roots of the plants are grown in a nutrient solution or in a medium such as perlite or gravel.

Types of Hydroponic farming;

  • Deep water culture.

Deep water culture (DWC) is a hydroponic method of plant production by means of suspending the plant roots in a solution of nutrient-rich, oxygenated water.

Drip system.

A drip system is an active hydroponic system. This means that it uses a pump to feed your plants with nutrients and water regularly.

Nutrient Film Technique (NFT) system.

Nutrient film technique (NFT) is a hydroponic technique where in a very shallow stream of water containing all the dissolved nutrients required for plant growth is re-circulated past the bare roots of plants in a watertight gully, also known as channels.

Wick System.

A wick system is a watering method for potted plants that uses a soft fabric string known as a wick. One end of the wick is buried in the soil, and the other end hangs into a pot, dish, or bucket of water. Water will flow up the wick and water the plant until the soil surrounding the plant is damp.

Ebb and Flow system.

An ebb and flow system, also known as a flood and drain system, is a popular hydroponic growing system where there's an intermittent water flow over plants grown in an inert medium.

Aeroponic system.

Aeroponic systems use water, liquid nutrients and a soilless growing medium to quickly and efficiently grow more colorful, tastier, better smelling and incredibly nutritious produce.
